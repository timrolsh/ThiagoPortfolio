Thiago Martinez - Music Portfolio

Living in the past
This track's title is from the sample that is heard throughout the beat (Living In the Past by Unnatural Funk Band). The creation of this beat was a happy mistake as there was a completely different direction in mind. The original goal was to make a loop only using the vocals. Upon trying that I wasn’t very happy with the results but I then reversed the sample to see if I would get any varying results. As I reversed it I was chopping up certain segments to see if they sounded right together and eventually found the guitar solo in reverse. I then rearranged parts of the solo to create the main loop and foundation of this beat.
os pais e filhos
The inspiration and soul of this beat comes directly from Rio de Janeiro. Tim Maia, and the movie City of God (Cidade de Deus) are both sampled in this project. Tim Maia’s song (Pais e filhos) is sampled for the first 30 seconds for an intro, and then transitioned into the chopped beat. The clips from the film can be heard in both the beginning, middle and end of the song. The beat features a chopped sample of Tim Maia singing “A vida e eso ai” which can be translated to “thats life”. I believe this chop represents what Rocket (the protagonist) has to go through throughout the film. Rocket’s situation never really changes from the beginning all the way until the end of the movie despite the events that happen. For that reason I believe these two samples go hand in hand together and make sense of one another.
Tsukihime
This track was to be another happy mistake as the intent of this song was to create a standard trap or hip hop beat but instead became something of a house record. Instead of adding basic trap dums I tried using a constant clap playing with some accent snares as well adding a hi-hat/brush effect to give it more groove. The drums I ended up using for the final take made me realize how much I can really expand into new genres in my own way. This track is one I believe would be played at some sort of boiler room set.
My Ordinary Life
This title of the track “My ordinary life” comes from the show“Nichijou” and the choice of the title stemmed from the fact of how carefree and lighthearted the song felt. The goal on this track was mostly to create something jazzy and relaxing while also having a catchy melody and some bouncy drums. The snare’s and 808’s are very basic but the hi-hat pattern creates most of the bounce and groove found in the beat. The takeaway from this track was that simplicity is sometimes key.
blackberry phone
The rather electronic and rhythmic melody playing throughout the song was originally a chord progression that I thought didn’t have much to it. Instead of creating a different progression I instead started playing around with different effects one of them being a plug in called gross beat. The effect had many different options for it but it basically took and played whatever it was used on in a different rhythm or pattern. After much time of playing with the result I got one that left me more than satisfied. The drums heard during sections of the song can be heard taking influence from jersey club as well as a subgenre of hiphop called “jerk”.The hey and go! Vocal samples you hear are what i think make the beat what it is, and without it I feel as if the track would lose its character. The atmosphere to me sounded like it would be something you heard in the early 2000’s hence the name of the track being “blackberry phone”.
Kuon
The title of this track is in reference to the horror game “kuon” which was released in 2004 for the ps2. The choice of this name came from the danceable but uncomfortable sound of the track. The atmospheric pad playing in the back as well as the strings create an unsettling feel to the track. While the drums throughout feel very upbeat and bouncy I wanted to keep the feeling of something mischievous.
Voltei
The goal for this track was to make something very catchy and upbeat for the listener. The electric piano and combination of bells perfectly create a very relaxing and almost video-gamey feel to the track. I used a loud and very bitcrushed 808 to give that bounce and retro feel. Just like “My ordinary life” I believe that simplicity is what's best sometimes.
saturn
The name of the track is to describe the exact kind of feel I was trying to go for in the song. The chords you hear are two electric pianos layered on another with one having a sort of delay effect. The piano combined with the many synths created a very space-like atmosphere.
